Basic First Aid Instructions

In case of emergency, follow these basic first aid steps while waiting for professional help:

  1. Check for responsiveness: Gently tap the person and ask loudly, "Are you okay?"
  2. Call for help: If the person is unresponsive, call 911 immediately or ask someone else to call.
  3. Check breathing: Look for chest movement, listen for breathing sounds, and feel for breath on your cheek.
  4. Start CPR if needed: If the person is not breathing, begin chest compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute.
  5. Control bleeding: Apply direct pressure to wounds with a clean cloth or bandage.
  6. Treat for shock: Keep the person lying down, elevate their legs if possible, and keep them warm.
  7. Do not move: Unless in immediate danger, do not move someone with a suspected spinal injury.
  8. Stay calm: Remain calm and reassure the person while waiting for emergency services.

Important: These are basic guidelines. Always seek professional medical help in emergencies.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Chest Pain

Severe chest pain, especially with shortness of breath, could indicate a heart attack. Seek immediate medical attention.

Severe Headache

Sudden, severe headache with vision changes or confusion may signal a stroke or other serious condition.

Difficulty Breathing

Severe shortness of breath, especially if sudden, requires immediate emergency care.

Severe Burns

Large burns, burns on face/hands, or chemical burns need immediate professional treatment.
